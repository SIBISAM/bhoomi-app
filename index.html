Legal Clarity

Built on solid legal ground

We know trust starts with legal certainty. Every Bhoomi agreement is a Leave and Licence — not a tenancy — reviewed by Kerala agricultural lawyers.

Reviewed by Kerala agricultural lawyers
  • All agreements are 11-month Leave and Licence
  • No tenancy rights accrue to the farmer
  • Auto-expiry at 11 months — owner retains full title
  • OTP-based digital signature for legal validity
  • Ready to migrate when Kerala Lease Bill passes
Yes. While the Kerala Agricultural Tenancy Act restricts formal long-term tenancy, short-term Leave and Licence agreements (11 months or less) are fully permissible and do not create tenancy rights.
A Leave and Licence grants the farmer a licence to cultivate for 11 months. It is explicitly not a tenancy. At the end of 11 months it expires automatically. The landowner retains full ownership throughout.
No. A Leave and Licence explicitly prevents any tenancy rights from accruing. Our agreements are clearly labelled "Leave and Licence" — never "tenancy" or "lease" — and a fresh agreement is signed each 11-month period.
The Kerala government tabled a Land Lease Legalisation Bill in August 2025. When it passes, Bhoomi will support seamless migration to formal lease agreements. Your data and agreement history carry forward — no action needed from you.
You need: (1) Patta document, (2) Survey number for the plot, (3) Property tax receipt (optional), (4) Clear photos of the land. You can upload scanned copies from abroad — no physical presence needed.
